Whether you’ve been learning Japanese for months or even years, we’ve all come across this question at some point: do I use は or が?
Maybe you’ve heard that the particle you use changes the focus of the sentence. Or maybe you read that it doesn’t really matter which one you use, even Japanese people can’t tell the difference. Or better yet, maybe you read a dissertation explaining the nuances that は and が give to the sentence as a whole. Whatever you’ve heard or read, knowing the difference between the two particles will definitely take you to that next step into advancing your understanding of Japanese.
